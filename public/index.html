About Me

I am a trauma-informed coach and breathwork practitioner with a diverse background and a non-linear career path. I consider myself a lifelong learner, a concept of which I have heard for the first time while going on a student exchange. I have a Bachelor in Business Management and Master in Intercultural Communication. I also studied Philosophy as a separate degree and although philosophy is still my passion I quit that study after 2.5 years. My education has not stopped with graduation and although fully employed, I keep on learning.
I am currently working as an HR Business Partner and have had various roles in startups and corporate environments. I did further education with Swiss employment law and completed full stack development bootcamp over pandemic and worked on software development projects. I love to change environments and from time to time I volunteer during cultural events like film and music festivals. Up until my move to Switzerland I thought I would like to travel all over the world and see as many different places as possible - it changed.
It was also the case with learning languages. Polish is my mother tongue and I learned English and French at school. There weren’t any native speakers around me back then and I was quite shocked when I moved to the UK in my early 20s. French as a phonetic language hasn’t been my thing and Spanish didn’t really catch my heart. I was very enthusiastic about learning Greek when moving to Greece and learning this language was so meaningful (do you know that Kosmos means people in greek?) and also its different alphabet seems like a nice adventure. And it was so! However lack of application in my live and wanting to get a job in a german speaking country resulted in adding German to my language repertoire.
